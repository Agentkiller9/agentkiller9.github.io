### Types of Security Audits — Like Inspecting Different Rooms in a Building

As I keep learning and diving deeper into auditing, one thing became clear:

> **_Not all audits are the same._**

Just like inspecting a building means checking the wiring, the plumbing, and the fire exits separately — **security audits** also come in different flavors depending on _what_ you’re evaluating.

Here’s a breakdown of the **main types of security audits**, explained in plain language and real-world examples.

### The Analogy: Security Audits Are Like Room-Specific Inspections

If your company were a building:

*   An **internal audit** checks how tidy your own room is
*   An **external audit** is like a landlord doing a surprise inspection
*   A **compliance audit** is the fire marshal checking that fire alarms and exits are up to code
*   A **technical audit** examines the wiring and plumbing behind the walls
*   A **network audit** tests how well your security gates and hallway doors are working
*   An **application audit** makes sure the appliances don’t start fires or leak data

Now let’s go through each one.

### 1\. Internal Audit

_Done by your own organization’s tea_m  
Checks if internal controls, access permissions, and policies are being followed

Example:  
An IT team audits user permissions and finds that interns still have access to old HR folders.

_Why it matters:_ It’s your chance to self-check before outsiders do.

### 2\. External Audit

_Performed by a third-party (objective and independent)_  
Provides an unbiased evaluation of your security posture and compliance

Example:  
An external firm conducts a PCI DSS audit to confirm your customer payment systems are secure.

_Why it matters:_ These carry weight — with regulators, partners, and customers.

### 3\. Compliance Audit

_Focused on legal and regulatory requirements_  
Makes sure you’re following frameworks like GDPR, HIPAA, or ISO 27001

Example:  
A healthcare org undergoes a HIPAA audit to ensure patient records are protected per federal law.

_Why it matters:_ Failing here can mean fines, lawsuits, or revoked certifications.

### 4\. Technical Audit

_Focused on the actual tech — configs, systems, software_  
Digs deep into how your IT environment is secured under the hood

Example:  
Auditors review firewall rules and discover outdated ports open to the internet.

_Why it matters:_ These audits give penetration testers valuable leads.

### 5\. Network Audit

_Targets the structure of your network itself_  
Looks at switches, routers, firewalls, and how data flows across the org

Example:  
A network audit finds that Telnet (insecure) is still being used instead of SSH.

_Why it matters:_ Poor network design = open invitation to attackers.

### 6\. Application Audit

_Focuses on software security_  
Evaluates login systems, input validation, code quality, and data handling

Example:  
Auditors find a web app vulnerable to SQL injection and XSS.

_Why it matters:_ Apps are where a lot of real-world attacks begin — this is your digital storefront.

### Final Thoughts

Studying auditing has taught me that **an audit isn’t one big test** — it’s made up of many smaller, specialized evaluations.

The more specific your audit type, the clearer your findings — and the easier it is to fix what matters.

Whether you’re an org preparing for an audit or a learner trying to understand the field, knowing the **types of audits** helps you ask better questions and build better defenses.
